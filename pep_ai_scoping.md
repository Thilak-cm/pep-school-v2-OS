PEP OS Scoping \| AI Features

Big picture PEP's observation app is becoming the home of the child's
qualitative history:

● spontaneous observations (already there), ● lessons given (about to be
logged), ● eventually photos/artifacts of work.

We want to layer AI on top so that:

1.  Teachers get a co-pilot for thinking about each child.
2.  Program leads and management get a command line to the school's
    lived reality.
3.  The system actively reduces blind spots instead of just storing what
    adults happen to notice.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Layer 1 -- Teacher co-pilot

## 1. Per-child "Last 6 Weeks" profile (with lesson summary & coverage strip)

**What it is**\
A one-tap profile per child that shows for a chosen window (default:
last 6 weeks): - 5--7 narrative bullets summarizing the last 6 weeks\
- Concrete examples tied to real observations ("On Nov 18, during...
he...")\
- A compact recent lesson summary

**Why it matters / how it helps** - Default "what's going on with this
child?" screen\
- Helps teachers, leads, management instantly understand:\
- how the child is working\
- what they were offered\
- where attention has been missing (coverage gaps)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Per-child AI chat thread (teacher ↔ AI ↔ data)

**What it is**\
Each child gets a dedicated AI chat. Teachers can ask: - "Should I be
worried about X's social behaviour?" - "What 2--3 next steps would you
suggest for their reading?" - "What questions should I ask parents in
our next meeting?"

AI answers using: - the child's observations & lesson history\
- developmental / Montessori intelligence (LLM)

AI can also ask back: - "I notice we have rich notes in English but
almost nothing in sports. Could you observe them in sports once and log
what you see?"

**Why it matters** - Scales AI thinking to every teacher\
- Turns the system into an active thinking partner\
- Surfaces blind spots ("we have no data on X in context Y")

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Early-risk detection & alerts (including "negative space")

**What it is**\
Weekly background job that flags:

**Classic risk patterns:** - frequent conflicts in multiple contexts\
- persistent refusal/avoidance\
- anxiety/perfectionism clusters\
- sensory/OT-type patterns (fatigue, handwriting pain, etc.)

**Negative-space patterns:** - behaviours absent compared to peers
(quiet absences)

Each alert includes: - reasons/features detected\
- 2--4 questions for teachers\
- suggested next steps

**Why it matters** - Shortens time from pattern → intervention\
- Surfaces loud issues + quiet absences\
- Gives coordinators a grounded "watch list"

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. Primary & Elementary "what comes next?" suggestions

AI proposes next steps based on Montessori sequences.

**Primary:**\
- language\
- math

**Elementary:**\
- enrichment: problem sets, small research projects, reading suggestions

**Why it matters** - Planning assistant for teachers\
- Prevents strong children from coasting

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. AI-drafted term reports

Automatically drafts reports using logged evidence.

**Why it matters** - Saves huge prep time\
- Enforces evidence-based reporting\
- Produces richer, more grounded reports

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6. Parent-meeting co-pilot

For scheduled parent meetings: - generates 3--5 key points\
- 3 anecdotes\
- suggested Montessori language\
- 2--3 parent questions\
- 2--3 suggestions for parents

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Layer 2 -- Leadership & coordination layer

## 1. Leadership query console -- children

One big text box where leaders can ask:

-   "Who has been struggling in the last one month?"\
-   "Who is shining in the adolescent program right now?"\
-   "Which primary children show repeated conflict at snack time?"\
-   "Show Grade 4 kids who are strong academically but socially
    isolated."\
-   "Which kids have had almost no observations in the last 3 weeks?"

System returns: - ranked list\
- 2--3 line explanation\
- links to profiles

**Why it matters** - Live, queryable view of the school\
- Makes it trivial to find children needing attention or recognition

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Leadership query console -- teachers

Leaders can ask:

-   "Which teachers log the highest proportion of challenging
    observations?"\
-   "Which teachers logged the least in the last 30 days?"\
-   "Which classrooms have the most conflict notes?"\
-   "Which teachers rarely log anything about social or emotional
    domains?"

Outputs: - teacher name + environment\
- metrics: volume, valence ratio, domain coverage\
- short interpretation

**Why it matters** - Helps identify where adults/environments need
support\
- Surfaces patterns: under-logging, negative logging, narrow focus

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Saved queries & periodic briefings

Any query can be saved as a briefing with: - frequency
(weekly/fortnightly/monthly)\
- time window\
- recipients

Examples: - "Who's been struggling in the last month?" → weekly\
- "Who is shining in each elementary environment?" → fortnightly\
- "Which teachers have unusually high challenge-only logging?" → monthly

System generates: - email / in-app briefing\
- top N children\
- explanations\
- notable patterns\
- links to profiles

**Why it matters** - Converts analytics from pull → push\
- Ensures leadership never misses important changes

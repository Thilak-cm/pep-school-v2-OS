# Guidelines Template — Primary (Ages 3-6)

> **Purpose:** This document defines the developmental areas, skills, and observable
> benchmarks that Coach Pepper uses to guide observations, interviews, and soul
> generation for students in the Primary program.
>
> **How to use:** Scan the discipline sections to identify relevant areas for a
> student. Use benchmarks as concrete indicators when observing, interviewing
> teachers, or generating the weekly soul narrative. Benchmarks are not checklists
> — they describe behaviors that may emerge at different paces for each child.
>
> **Editing:** This copy evolves with the student. You may add, remove, or annotate
> sections. Add custom notes under any heading with `> Note:` blockquotes.

---


## Personal, Social & Emotional Skills

### Motivation (Personal Development)

- Chooses appropriately challenging tasks across domains.
- Enjoys independently taking on new challenges.
- Works with concentration when guided towards challenging work.

### Independence & Choice (Personal Development)

- Selects and uses materials, activities and resources independently
- Makes appropriate choices about learning and playing in a variety of contexts.
- Independently executes the work cycle from start to finish (including winding up)
- Able to take care of own personal things without help

### Self-Control (Personal Development)

- Shows awareness that some things are theirs, some things are shared, and some things belong to other people
- Shows understanding of daily routines
- Co-operates independently with defined boundaries
- Inhibits own actions/behaviours when it is inappropriate
- Tolerates delay when needs are not immediately met, and understands wishes may not always be met.

### Self-Confidence & Self-Awareness (Personal Development)

- Discusses learning with adult, and describes likes and dislikes.
- Celebrates and values individual achievements
- Describes self in positive terms and talks about abilities.
- Shows confidence in asking adults for help.
- Outgoing towards unfamiliar people and confident in new social situations.

### Determination and Resilience (Personal Development)

- Stays on task that may at first seem challenging, to achieve success.
- Expresses some emotions appropriately, for example, happy/sad and understands how they can affect behaviour.
- Is learning how to be a good winner and cope appropriately with losing.

### Responsibility and Leadership (Personal Development)

- Accepts direction with co-operation from an adult or peer.
- Enjoys responsibility of carrying out small tasks.
- Adopts different roles when working as part of a group.
- Guides and encourages other peers in their work.

### Social Interaction & Communication (Social Development)

- Plays cooperatively with a familiar adult, e.g. rolling a ball back and forth.
- Shows interest in others’ play and joins in collaborative play.
- Plays in a group, extending and elaborating play ideas, e.g. building up a role-play activity with other children.
- Initiates play, offering cues to peers to join them.
- Initiates conversations with peers.
- Uses appropriate words and/or body language to express thoughts and feelings with peers
- Is developing the ability to know when to listen and when to talk when interacting with others.
- Attends to and takes account of what others say (to continue conversation)
- Explains own knowledge and understanding, and asks appropriate questions of others.
- Develops special friendships with peers.
- Takes steps to resolve conflicts with other children, e.g. finding a compromise.

### Managing Feelings (Emotional Development)

- Growing sense of will and determination sometimes result in feelings of anger and frustration which are difficult to handle, e.g. may have tantrums.
- Can express their own feelings such as sad, happy, cross, scared, worried.
- Able to comfort and settle themselves after emotional moments.

### Empathy, Respect & Tolerance (Emotional Development)

- Aware that some actions can hurt or harm others.
- Shows affection and concern for adults who are special to them.
- Tries to help or give comfort to peers when they are distressed.
- Accepts the needs of others and can take turns and share resources.
- Able to negotiate and solve problems without aggression, e.g. when someone has taken their material


## HEALTH AND WELLBEING

### Eating & Food Choices (Food and Health)

- Feeds self competently with spoon.
- Drinks well without spilling.
- Gets ready independently to have food (for example: washes hands, organises lunch boxes neatly)
- Demonstrates an understanding of basic food hygiene
- Eats socially with others.
- Recognises that we eat different foods at different times of the day.
- Identifies and tastes a range of basic foods
- Recognises that eating more of some types of foods and less of others is good for health.
- Aware of how much fluid should be consumed in a day.

### Self-care: Hygiene & Safety (Food and Health)

- Aware of how cleanliness, hygiene and safety can affect health and wellbeing.
- Clearly communicates their need for the washroom
- Attend independently to their toileting needs in the washroom
- Performs daily hygiene routines, for example, hand washing before meals or after use of toilet
- Becoming aware of extra-care when sick (for example, running nose, sneezing)
- Shows understanding of the need for safety when tackling new challenges, considers and manages some risks.
- Works safely when moving heavy material or while using sharp objects such as scissors or knives

### Physical activity and health (Physical)

- Demonstrates different ways of being active, for example, energetic play.
- Gives reasons why being active is good for health.

### Gross Physical Competencies (Physical)

- Shows awareness of personal space (i.e. where body ends and space begins).
- Shows control of personal space and body parts when moving.
- Holds balance in various situations and maintains balance when moving.
- Shows awareness of body parts and body positions when performing a range of different movements.
- Performs basic components of movement, for example, run, jump, gallop, transfer of weight from one foot to another.
- Climbs stairs on alternate feet without support
- Moves safely at different speeds, levels and directions with others in a designated space.
- Moves with purpose across contexts
- Links movements together (moves body or parts of body in order).
- Demonstrates how to use repeated patterns of movement to create simple sequences, for example, one foot to two feet jumping.
- Responds with movement, for example, jump-clap-turn to recognised rhythm, beat, music, words.
- Is beginning to perform movement skills in sequence, for example, catch an object with two hands.
- Is beginning to demonstrate eye/hand and eye/foot co-ordination required for movement skills, for example, pass object from one hand to the other.

### Physical Fitness (Physical)

- Sustains energetic levels of play/activity.
- Identifies different ways to be physically active.
- Describes how the body changes when engaged in moderate to vigorous activity.
- Moves at different speeds – slowly, steadily and quickly.
- Moves parts of the body at different speeds.
- Starts and stops quickly.
- Is developing postural control when performing physical actions.
- Moves in control and maintains shape.
- Holds body weight/position of stillness for short periods of time.
- Moves freely across a full range of movements.

### Fine Motor Skills (Physical)

- Shows a preference for a dominant hand.
- Uses one-handed tools and equipment, e.g. makes snips in paper with child scissors.
- Shows control in holding objects
- Pours dry items without spilling
- Pours water without spilling
- Shows control in transferring items with tools (such as spoons).
- Holds pencil between thumb and two fingers, no longer using whole-hand grasp.
- Holds pencil near point between first two fingers and thumb and uses it with good control.
- Draws simple shapes such as circles and lines with support (stencils / insets)

### Cognitive skills (Cognitive)

- Focuses on tasks and pays attention to relevant stimuli, for example, instructions from a educator.
- Recognises external cues that need an immediate response, for example, starting and stopping an activity.
- Repeats tasks in the correct sequence.
- Follows one-step simple instructions.
- Follows two-step simple instructions.
- Listens to and makes sense of three (or more) pieces of information.
- Works out the order for dealing with information (or tasks).
- Makes decisions independently in the context of simple tasks or in response to an input.
- Uses prior knowledge, and identifies key information to help form a solution.
- Demonstrates imagination in different situations (work or play).


## NUMERACY AND MATHEMATICS

### Basic Understanding of Numbers (Number, Money and Measure)

- Recites some number names in sequence.
- Uses some language of quantities, such as ‘more’ and ‘a lot’.
- Uses some number names and number language spontaneously.
- Compares two groups of objects with appropriate language ('equal', 'greater', 'lesser')
- Selects a small number of objects from a group when asked, for example, ‘please give me one’, ‘please give me two’.
- Matches numerals and quantity correctly (upto 10)
- Orders all numbers forwards and backwards within the range 0 - 20.
- Identifies the number before, the number after and missing numbers in a sequence within 20.
- Uses ordinal numbers in real life contexts, for example, ‘I am third in the line’.
- Uses the language of before, after and in-between.
- Understands that zero means there is none of a particular quantity and is represented by the numeral 0.
- Identifies small numbers as "even" or "odd"
- Constructs a 4 digit number & represents it accurately using the principles of the decimal system
- Understands the relationship between the hierarchies of the decimal system (eg 10 units make 1 ten, 10 hunderds make 1 thousand and so on)
- Understands the terminology of numbers between 10-20 (eleven, twelve,...) and can represent the quantities accurately.
- Understands the terminology for multiples of 10 (twenty, thirty...) and can represent the quantities accurately.

### Estimation and rounding (Number, Money and Measure)

- Recognises the number of objects in a group, without counting (subitising) and uses this information to estimate the number of objects in other groups.
- Checks estimates by counting.
- Demonstrates skills of estimation in the contexts of number and measure using relevant vocabulary, including less than, longer than, more than and the same.

### Number Operations (Number, Money and Measure)

- Shows understanding that a group of things changes in quantity when something is added or taken away.
- Merges concrete quantities accurately, performing the addition operation without carry
- Merges concrete quantities accurately, performing the addition operation with carry
- Adds single digit numbers mentally
- Understands the commutative law of addition (a+b = b+a)
- Subtracts concrete quantities accurately, performing the subtraction operation without carry
- Subtracts concrete quantities accurately, performing the subtraction operation with carry
- Subtracts single digit numbers mentally
- Mutiplies concrete quantities accurately
- Multiplies single digit numbers mentally
- Divides concrete quantities accurately
- Uses appropriately the mathematical symbols for addition and subtraction
- Uses appropriately the mathematical symbols for multiplication and division
- In practical activities and discussion, beginning to use the vocabulary involved in math operations
- Shows an interest in number problems.
- Begins to identify own mathematical problems based on own interests and fascinations.

### Fractions, Decimal fractions and Percentages (Number, Money and Measure)

- Splits a whole into smaller parts and explains that equal parts are the same size.
- Uses appropriate vocabulary to describe halves.
- Shares out a group of items equally into smaller groups.

### Money (Number, Money and Measure)

- Identifies all coins to Rs 10.
- Applies addition and subtraction skills and uses Re 1, Rs 2, Rs 5 and Rs 10 coins to pay the exact value for items to Rs 10.

### Time (Number, Money and Measure)

- Links daily routines and personal events to time sequences.
- Names the days of the week in sequence
- Names the months of the year in sequence
- Reads analogue clock times and represents them on the clock face.

### Measurement (Number, Money and Measure)

- Shares relevant experiences in which measurements of lengths, heights, mass and capacities are used, for example, in baking.
- Describes common objects using appropriate measurement language, including tall, heavy and empty.
- Compares and describes lengths, heights, mass and capacities using everyday language, including longer, shorter, taller, heavier, lighter, more and less.
- Estimates, then measures, the length, height, mass and capacity of familiar objects using a range of appropriate non-standard units.

### Patterns and relationships (Number, Money and Measure)

- Explores, recognises and continues simple patterns involving objects & shapes.
- Explores, recognises and continues simple number patterns.
- Finds missing numbers on a number line within the range 0 - 20.

### Data & Analysis (Number, Money and Measure)

- Asks simple questions to collect data for a specific purpose.
- Collects and organises objects for a specific purpose.
- Applies counting skills to ask and answer questions and makes relevant choices and decisions based on the data.
- Uses knowledge of colour, shape, size and other properties to match and sort items in a variety of different ways.
- Interprets simple graphs, charts and signs and demonstrates how they support planning, choices and decision making.

### Shapes, Objects and Size (Shape, Size and Position)

- Recognises, describes and sorts common 2D shapes and 3D objects according to various criteria, for example, straight, round, flat and curved.
- Fits shapes into matching spaces on inset boards, materials or jigsaw puzzles
- Sorts and categories objects according to properties such as shape or size
- Uses blocks to create simple structures and arrangements.
- Identifies simple shapes and patterns in pictures.
- Shows awareness of similarities of shapes in the environment.
- Talks about the shapes of everyday objects, e.g. ‘round’ and ‘tall’.
- Uses mathematical names for ‘flat’ 2D shapes
- Uses mathematical names for ‘solid’ 3D shapes .
- Orders two or three items by dimension
- Orders more than three items by dimension
- Uses familiar objects and common shapes to create and recreate patterns and build models.

### Position & Direction (Shape, Size and Position)

- Understands and correctly uses the language of position and direction such as in front, behind, above, below, left, right, forwards and backwards,
- Identifies, describes and creates symmetrical pictures with one line of symmetry.


## COMMUNICATION AND LANGUAGE

### Enjoyment and Choice (Listening  and Attention)

- Joins & listens intently during song & rhyme time
- Joins & concentrates intently during story time
- Listens to others one to one or in small groups, when conversation interests them.
- Accepts invitations to 1-1 presentations and listens with focus
- Accepts invitations to 1-1 group sessions and listens with focus
- Accepts invitations to whole class sessions and listens with focus

### Tools for listening (Listening  and Attention)

- Makes an attempt to use appropriate body language when listening to others, for example, eye contact.
- Makes an attempt to take turns when listening and talking in a variety of contexts.
- Recognises and responds to many familiar sounds, e.g. turning to a knock on the door, looking at or going to the door.
- Listens and responds to aduts appropriately (for e.g., on receiving instructions)
- Listens and responds to peers appropriately
- Listens to and extends conversations logically

### Understanding, analysing and evaluating (Understanding)

- Able to follow a story with pictures or props
- Able to follow a story without pictures or props.
- Identifies new or interesting information from spoken texts.
- Recalls and anticipates key events and phrases in rhymes and stories.
- Asks and answers questions about texts to show and support understanding.
- Shows understanding of prepositions such as ‘under’, ‘on top’, ‘behind’ by carrying out an action or selecting correct picture.
- Understands humour, e.g. nonsense rhymes, jokes.
- Makes simple predictions about texts (e.g: what comes next).

### Questions & Instructions (Understanding)

- Responds to instructions involving a single step (do X)
- Responds to instructions involving a two-part sequence (do X, then Y)
- Responds to instructions involving a three-part sequence (do X and Y, then Z after some time)
- Demonstrates understanding of ‘why’ and ‘how’ questions.

### Functional Speech (Talking)

- Copies and re-uses familiar expressions, e.g. ‘Oh dear’, ‘All gone’.
- Constructs original short sentences (two-three words long)
- Constructs complex sentences (using "and", "because" etc.)
- Recounts experiences, stories and events in a logical sequence for different purposes.
- Uses a range of tenses (e.g. play, playing, will play, played).
- Acquires new vocabulary rapidly and is able to use them in communicating.
- Uses different types of everyday words (nouns, verbs and adjectives, e.g. banana, go, sleep, hot).
- Use word endings (e.g. going, cats) appropriately
- Asks questions in sentence form

### Expressive Speech (Talking)

- Talks about people and things that are not present.
- Uses language as a powerful means of widening contacts, sharing feelings, experiences and thoughts.
- Uses language to imagine and recreate roles and experiences in play situations.
- Uses intonation, rhythm and phrasing to make the meaning clear to others.
- Shares ideas with a wider audience, for example, group or class.
- Uses new vocabulary and phrases in different contexts, for example, when expressing ideas and feelings or discussing a text.
- Introduces a storyline or narrative into their conversation or play.


## LITERACY & ENGLISH

### Enjoyment and choice (Reading)

- Chooses a story book or other texts for enjoyment
- Chooses texts and practices reading independently
- Spontaneously identifies and reads words in the environment

### Tools for reading (Reading)

- Hears and says patterns in words.
- Recognises familiar words and signs such as own name and advertising logos.
- Looks at books independently.
- Handles books carefully.
- Hears and says patterns in words.
- Hears and says the different single sounds made by letters.
- Hears and says blends/sounds made by a combination of letters.
- Reads from left to right and top to bottom.
- Uses knowledge of sounds, letters and patterns to read words.
- Uses knowledge of sight vocabulary/tricky words to read familiar words in context.
- Reads aloud familiar texts with attention to simple punctuation.
- Uses context clues to support understanding of different texts.

### Finding and using information (Reading)

- Finds information in a text to learn new things.
- Shows an awareness of a few features of fiction and non-fiction texts when using/choosing texts for particular purposes.

### Understanding, analysing and evaluating (Reading)

- Relates information and ideas from a text to personal experiences.
- Asks and answers questions about events and ideas in a text.
- Answers questions to help predict what will happen next.
- Shares thoughts and feelings about stories and other texts in different ways.
- Retells familiar books in different ways, for example, role play, puppets and/or drawings.

### Enjoyment and choice (Writing)

- Spontaneously writes identifiable letters to communicate meaning, representing some sounds correctly and in sequence.
- Writes for enjoyment, exploring patterns and sounds, in a range of play, imaginative and real contexts.

### Tools for writing (Writing)

- Constructs words with the movable alphabets
- Constructs sentences with the movable alphabets
- Spontaneously writes letters on chalkboards or paper
- Uses a pencil with control and confidence.
- Forms lowercase letters legibly.
- Writes identifiable letters to communicate meaning, representing some sounds correctly and in sequence.
- Writes words from left to right.
- Leaves a space between words when writing.
- Makes an attempt to use a capital letter and a full stop.
- Makes an attempt to spell familiar words correctly.

### Creating Texts (Writing)

- Writes to convey ideas, messages and information
- Writes to reflect own experiences and feelings using appropriate vocabulary to convey meaning.

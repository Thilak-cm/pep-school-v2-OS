# Guidelines Template — Elementary (Ages 6-12)

> **Purpose:** This document defines the developmental areas, skills, and observable
> benchmarks that Coach Pepper uses to guide observations, interviews, and soul
> generation for students in the Elementary program.
>
> **How to use:** Scan the discipline sections to identify relevant areas for a
> student. Use benchmarks as concrete indicators when observing, interviewing
> teachers, or generating the weekly soul narrative. Benchmarks are not checklists
> — they describe behaviors that may emerge at different paces for each child.
>
> **Editing:** This copy evolves with the student. You may add, remove, or annotate
> sections. Add custom notes under any heading with `> Note:` blockquotes.

---


## Personal, Social & Emotional Skills

### Motivation (Personal Development)

- Chooses appropriately challenging tasks across domains
- Enjoys independently taking on new challenges
- Recognises and responds to both internal and external motivation to set targets, achieve personal goals and improve performance.
- Chooses appropriately challenging tasks across domains; Enjoys independently taking on new challenges
- Is self-motivated in challenging situations, and demonstrates positive effort
- Sets and acts upon personal goals based on knowledge and understanding of what it means to perform well.

### Independence & Choice (Personal Development)

- Selects and uses materials, activities and resources independently; Able to take care of own personal things without help
- Independently executes work from start to finish, and achieves increasing rates of completion

### Self-Control (Personal Development)

- Inhibits own actions and behaviours when they are inappropriate
- Demonstrates capacity to wait for one's turn across contexts
- Developing planning and organisational skills.
- Developing planning and organisational skills which are conducive to learning

### Self-Confidence & Self-Awareness (Personal Development)

- Discusses learning with adult, and describes likes and dislikes.
- Outgoing towards unfamiliar people and confident in new social situations.
- Demonstrates self-reliance when faced with challenges in familiar and unfamiliar practice and performance situations.
- Initiates and works cooperatively with others providing support and encouragement.
- Self-assesses and acts as a peer assessor to provide constructive feedback to improve performance.
- Celebrates, values and uses achievements as part of development and progress.

### Determination & Resilience (Personal Development)

- Stays on task that may at first seem challenging, to achieve success
- Is learning how to be a good winner and cope appropriately with losing
- Demonstrates understanding of the positive link between effort, perseverance, and personal achievement.
- Is learning how to be a good winner and cope appropriately with losing; Recognises a variety of emotions and is developing the ability to manage them appropriately.

### Managing Feelings (Social & Emotional Development)

- Can express their own feelings such as sad, happy, scared, worried,
- Able to comfort and settle themselves when required.

### Social Interaction & Communication (Social & Emotional Development)

- Listens to and responds to the ideas, thoughts and feelings of others appropriately. Uses appropriate words and/or body language to express ideas, thoughts and feelings.
- Is developing the ability to know when to listen and when to talk when interacting with others.

### Respect & Tolerance (Social & Emotional Development)

- Is aware of ideas, thoughts and feelings of others.
- Shares with others and shows consideration during group work / play.
- Demonstrates how to include others when completing tasks or playing together.
- Listens to and responds to the ideas, thoughts and feelings of others, and is developing negotiation skills when dealing with conflicts.
- Demonstrates how to include others when completing tasks or playing together; contributes to inclusion in the community

### Responsibility and Leadership (Social & Emotional Development)

- Accepts direction with co-operation from an adult or peer
- Enjoys responsibility of carrying out tasks on behalf of the community
- Guides and encourages other peers in their work
- Adopts a variety of roles in group situations
- Guides and encourages other peers in their work spontaneously
- Demonstrates understanding of a leadership role


## HEALTH AND WELLBEING

### Gross Physical Competencies (Physical)

- Moves safely at different speeds, levels and directions with others in a designated space. Moves freely across a full range of movements.
- Moves with purpose demonstrating balance, control and rhythm. Performs movement skills in sequence.
- Demonstrates eye/hand and eye/foot co-ordination required for movement skills, for example, track the flight of the ball with the eyes, then catch it or kick it
- Links and orders a series of movements with and without equipment to perform a sequence, for example, hopscotch.

### Physical Fitness (Physical)

- Participates in moderate to vigorous physical activity and sustains a level of activity that provides challenge.
- Demonstrates short bursts of fast movement from stillness.
- Shows postural control when starting, stopping and changing direction. Uses a full range of movement to perform actions effectively.
- Moves at different speeds and is able to maintain balance whilst changing direction quickly.

### Cognitive skills (Cognitive)

- Focuses on task and pays attention to stimuli, for example, instructions from a educator.
- Focuses attention in more demanding situations, for example, working with a partner or in a small group.
- Follows two-step simple instructions; Listens to and makes sense of three (or more) pieces of information
- Repeats tasks in the correct sequence.
- Plans and creates a sequence of actions for own work
- Demonstrates flair, originality, and imagination.
- Follows two-step simple instructions; Listens to and makes sense of three (or more) pieces of information; Makes appropriate decisions when presented with two or three different options and can explain why.
- Demonstrates adaptability when finding different solutions to solve problems.


## NUMERACY AND MATHEMATICS

### Basic Understanding of Numbers & Number Operations (Numbers, Operations & Analysis)

- Identifies and understands place value in a 4-digit number
- Identifies and understands place value in a 7-digit number
- Performs mental addition and subtraction operations with numbers from 1-10
- Performs mental multiplication and division operations upto the tables of 10
- Demonstrates understanding of addition & subtraction operations with large numbers (using materials)
- Performs addition and subtraction operations with large numbers on paper
- Demonstrates understanding of multiplication operations with large numbers (using materials)
- Performs simple multiplication operations on paper using the long multiplication approach (e.g 1345 X 6)
- Performs advanced multiplication operations on paper using the long multiplication approach (e.g 1345 X 126)
- Demonstrates understanding of division operations with large dividends (using materials)
- Performs division operations with large dividends on paper using the long division approach
- Comprehends and solves single-operation word problems
- Comprehends and solves multi-step word problems
- Describes and solves number sequences / patterns using addition, subtraction, doubling, halving, etc
- Performs all mental operations with numbers from 1-10
- Performs multiplication operations on paper using the long multiplication approach
- Demonstrates understanding of multiplication operations with large multiplicands (using materials)
- Comprehends and solves multi-step word problems featuring basic operations

### Analysing Numbers (Numbers, Operations & Analysis)

- Identifies multiples of numbers
- Identifies factors of numbers
- Uses knowledge of multiples to calculate lowest common multiples (LCM) of pairs of numbers
- Uses knowledge of factors to calculate highest common factors (HCF) of pairs of numbers
- Demonstrates an understanding of prime numbers
- Uses knowledge of prime numbers to identify prime factors of a given number
- Demonstrates an understanding of the commutative laws of addition and multiplication (6 + 4 = 4 + 6)
- Demonstrates an understanding of the distributive law of multiplication ( 7 X 11 = 7 X 10 + 7 X 1)
- Demonstrates an understanding of facts of divisibility relating to simple divisors like 2, 5 and 10
- Demonstrates an understanding of facts of divisibility relating to more advanced divisors like 3, 6 and 9
- Identifies multiples and factors of numbers
- Shows understanding of prime numbers and prime factorization
- Calculates LCM and HCF of pairs of numbers
- Demonstrates an understanding of the commutative and distributive laws
- Shows awareness of the concept of squaring numbers and square roots
- Shows awareness of the concept of cubing numbers and cube roots
- Shows awareness of the concept of exponents
- Estimates and calculates square roots
- Demonstrates awareness of the concept of negative numbers
- Performs mathematical operations with negative numbers
- Demonstrates awareness of numbers in different bases
- Converts numbers from one number base to another

### Fractions (Numbers, Operations & Analysis)

- Uses the correct notation for common fractions, for example, 1/2 , 2/3 and 5/8 .
- Accurately identifies equivalent fractions, for example 1/2 and 2/4
- Expresses fractions in their simplest reduced form.
- Converts between improper and mixed fractions
- Adds and subtracts fractions with the same denominator
- Adds and subtracts fractions with different denominators using materials
- Adds and subtracts fractions with different denominators on paper
- Multiplies fractions with whole numbers
- Multiplies fractions with fractions
- Divides fractions with whole numbers
- Divides fractions with fractions
- Divides whole numbers with fractions
- Solves word problems with single operations involving fractions
- Solves word problems with multiple operations involving fractions
- Accurately identifies equivalent fractions; reduces fractions to their simplest forms
- Adds and subtracts fractions with same and different denominators
- Multiplies fractions with fractions and whole numbers
- Divides fractions by fractions and whole numbers
- Comprehends and solves multi-step word problems featuring fractions

### Analysing Shapes (Geometry)

- Recognises and names a range of simple shapes
- Shows awareness of simple geometrical terms like points, straight lines and curved lines.
- Estimates and measures the length of familiar objects using a ruler
- Uses knowledge of relationships between units of measure to make simple conversions, for example, 1 m 58 cm = 158 cm.
- Shows awareness of the concept of angles
- Uses mathematical language to describe and classify a range of angles (such as acute/obtuse)
- Measures angles using a protractor
- Shows awareness of different quadrilaterals and classifies them
- Shows awareness of different triangles and classifies them by sides and angles
- Names and identifies a variety of pairs of lines, such as parallel and perpendicular lines
- Names and analyses different kinds of angles (exterior, interior, corresponding, alternate, supplementary, complementary etc)
- Accurately constructs a variety of simple geometrical shapes using ruler and compass
- Accurately performs advanced geometric constructions such as medians & perpendicular bisectors
- Calculates the perimeter of simple straight sided 2D shapes
- Shows awareness of the parts of a circle
- Calculates the circumference of a circle with a given radius
- Identifies symmetry in patterns, pictures, nature and 2D shapes.
- Understands the concepts of congruence and similarity
- Understands the concept of geometric equivalence
- Estimates and measures the length of familiar objects using a ruler, and measures angles using a protractor

### Data Analysis (Information Handling)

- Asks simple questions to collect data for a specific purpose.
- Uses a variety of different methods to represent data (for example, as block graphs, bar graphs, and Venn diagrams)
- Interprets simple graphs and charts to arrive at conclusions

### Select and communicate processes and solutions (Mathematical Reasoning)

- Makes important decisions about which operations to choose when solving a word problem
- Uses frequent opportunities to discuss their mathematical thinking with their peers and teachers
- Is able to explain why they have chosen a particular process as it demonstrates their understanding of the task, question or assessment
- Regularly works in pairs and groups to learn with and from each other to refine their strategies

### Determine the reasonableness of a solution (Mathematical Reasoning)

- Seeks to find short-cut solutions using mathematical knowledge
- Uses estimation and rounding to estimate and check the reasonableness of a solution
- Considers the context of the question when determining the reasonableness of the solution

### Decimals, Percentages, Ratios (Numbers, Operations & Analysis)

- Accurately reads and writes decimal numbers (upto 1 millionth)
- Understands the relationship between decimals and simple fractions (for example, 0.5 = 1/2)
- Adds and subtracts decimal numbers
- Multiplies decimals with decimals and whole numbers
- Divides decimals by decimals
- Understands the concept of the percentage of a quantity
- Understands the concept of ratio
- Relates the concept of ratio with percentage and fractions
- Demonstrates the ability to solve word problems involving decimals, percentanges and/or ratios

### Area (Geometry)

- Understands the concept of area of a 2D shape
- Understands the derivation of the area of a rectangle
- Understands the derivation of the area of a triangle
- Understands the derivation of the area of larger polygons (like hexagons)
- Applies conceptual understanding to derive the area of any rectilinear shape
- Understands the derivation of the area of a circle
- Comprehends and solves problems on calculating area of different shapes
- Derives the surface area of faces of a 3D solid

### Volume (Geometry)

- Comprehends the concept of volume
- Understands the derivation of volume of various 3D solids
- Comprehends and solves problems on calculating volumes of various solids

### Data Analysis & Statistics (Information Handling)

- Asks simple questions to collect data for a specific purpose
- Extracts key information from a variety of data sets including charts, diagrams, bar graphs and tables.
- Uses a variety of different methods to represent data (for example, as block graphs, bar graphs, and Venn diagrams)
- Includes a suitable title, simple labelling on both axes and an appropriate scale
- Draws conclusions about the reliability of data taking into account, for example, the author, the audience, the scale and sample size used.
- Uses a variety of statistical values like mean, median and mode to interpret data

### Probability (Information Handling)

- Uses the language of probability accurately to describe the likelihood of simple events occurring, for example equal chance; fifty-fifty; one in two, two in three; percentage chance; and as a fraction
- Plans and carries out simple experiments involving chance with repeated trials, for example, ‘what is the probability of throwing a six if you throw a die fifty times?’
- Uses data to predict the outcome of a simple experiment.
- Solves simple word problems involving probability

### Basic understanding (Algebra)

- Understands that numbers can be replaced by letters, pictures or symbols and uses this to solve problems
- Collects like terms to simplify algebraic expressions
- Evaluates algebraic equations using substitution
- Solves simple linear algebraic equations
- Solves a system of linear equations
- Factorises algebraic expressions

### Applications (Algebra)

- Generates algebraic expressions across different contexts (for example, the nth term of a sequence)
- Derives and uses common arithmetic formulae (square of a binomial/trinomial, cube of a binomial/trinomial, etc)
- Derives and uses general formulas for areas of different geometric figures
- Plots algebraic expressions on a graph
- Represents lines in an algebraic form


## COMMUNICATION & LANGUAGE (ENGLISH)

### Enjoyment and Choice (Listening & Attention)

- Accepts invitations to large group sessions and listens with focus
- Accepts invitations to small group sessions and listens with focus
- Listens to others one to one or in small groups, when conversation interests them

### Tools for listening (Listening & Attention)

- Uses appropriate body language when listening to others (for e.g., eye contact)
- Makes an attempt to take turns when listening and talking in a variety of contexts
- Listens to adults and peers closely and responds relevantly

### Understanding, analysing and evaluating (Understanding)

- Understands and responds to oral lessons or spoken texts, and identifies new or interesting information.
- Answers questions to show and support understanding; Makes simple predictions (e.g: what comes next) that demonstrate understanding.
- Responds to instructions involving a single step, a two-part sequence (do X, then Y) and a three-part sequence (do X and Y, then Z)

### Tools for Talking (Talking)

- Responds relevantly to questions from others. Extends conversations logically.
- Volunteers to share ideas and speak in front of a small group audience
- Volunteers to share ideas and speak in front of large group audience
- Takes turns and contributes at the appropriate time when engaging with others in a variety of contexts.
- Structures thoughts logically while presenting to a group
- Applies a few techniques (verbal and non-verbal) when presenting, for example, eye contact, expression and/or body language.

### Functional Speech (Talking)

- Copies and re-uses familiar expressions, e.g. ‘Sit here’, ‘Thank you’; Constructs original short sentences and complex sentences (using "and", "because" etc.)
- Acquires new vocabulary rapidly and is able to use them in communicating; Recounts experiences, stories and events in a logical sequence for different purposes
- Uses a range of tenses (e.g. play, playing, will play, played); Uses different types of everyday words (nouns, verbs and adjectives); Use word endings (e.g. going, cats) appropriately
- Constructs original short sentences and complex sentences (using "and", "because" etc.)

### Expressive Speech (Talking)

- Talks clearly to others in different contexts, sharing feelings, ideas and thoughts.
- Communicates and shares stories or other spoken texts orally, using rhythm, tone and pacing to make the meaning clear
- Uses new vocabulary and phrases in different contexts, for example, when expressing ideas and feelings or discussing a text.
- Uses rich vocabulary and phrases in different spoken contexts, for example, when expressing ideas and feelings or discussing a text.

### Enjoyment & choice (Writing)

- Actively chooses writing tasks and writes for enjoyment in a variety of contexts

### Tools for writing (Writing)

- Uses a pencil with increasing control and confidence. Forms lowercase letters legibly.
- Attempts to spell familiar words correctly.
- Uses basic punctuation (capital letters, full stops)
- Constructs grammatically accurate sentences in written work.
- Organizes written work in a logical manner
- Uses relevant and/or interesting vocabulary in written work.

### Creating Texts (Writing)

- Writes to convey ideas, messages and information
- Writes to share own experiences and feelings
- Creates own creative texts, for example, stories, poems and plays
- Selects, organises and conveys information in different ways.

### Enjoyment and choice (Reading)

- Chooses a story book or other texts for enjoyment; Chooses texts and practices reading independently; Spontaneously identifies and reads words in the environment
- Accepts invitations to large group sessions and listens with focus
- Accepts invitations to small group sessions and listens with focus
- Listens to others one to one or in small groups, when conversation interests them

### Tools for reading (Reading)

- Identifies the different single sounds made by letters.
- Identifies blends/sounds made by a combination of letters.
- Uses knowledge of sounds, letters and patterns to read words.
- Uses knowledge of sight vocabulary/tricky words to read familiar words in context.
- Reads aloud familiar texts with attention to simple punctuation.
- Uses context clues to support understanding of different texts.

### Finding and using information (Reading)

- Finds information in a text to learn new things.
- Shows an awareness of a few features of fiction and non-fiction texts when using/choosing texts for particular purposes.

### Understanding &  analysing (Reading)

- Asks and answers questions about the content of a text and to help predict what happens next, after reading
- Contributes to discussions about events, characters and ideas relevant to the text. Relates information and ideas from a text to personal experiences, after reading

## Natural Sciences & Social Sciences

### Enjoyment & Choice (Basic Scientific & Experimental Skills)

- Demonstrates natural curiosity in conducting scientific experiments
- Demonstrates natural curiosity in the lives of people around the world, both today and over time (history)
- Demonstrates natural curiosity about physical geography (countries, maps, rivers, lakes, etc)

### Skills of scientifically literate citizens (Basic Scientific & Experimental Skills)

- Talks about science, showing developing understanding of risks and benefits, and listens to the views of others on scientific topics.
- Demonstrates awareness of the importance of respecting living things and of managing the Earth’s resources responsibly.
- Demonstrates a developing understanding of science in the world around them.

### Scientific Analysis (Basic Scientific & Experimental Skills)

- Makes predictions about the scientific investigation/enquiry being planned.
- Observes and collects information and makes measurements using appropriate equipment and units.
- Organises data and information and identifies significant patterns and relationships, interpreting findings
- Identifies and discusses additional knowledge or understanding gained.
- Reports learning in writing, orally or visually

### Enjoyment & Choice (Biology)

- Demonstrates natural curiosity about the plant world (botany)
- Demonstrates natural curiosity about the animal world (zoology)
- Cares for plants and animals in the school environment
- Demonstrates natural curiosity in the lives of people around the world, both today and over time (history)
- Demonstrates natural curiosity about physical geography (countries, maps, rivers, lakes, etc)

### Zoology (Biology)

- Shows awareness of the historical evolution of animals on earth
- Shows increasing awareness of animals found in India and across the world
- Demonstrates knowledge of the external parts & functions of a range of animals
- Building a basic awareness of scientific classification of animals
- Shows awareness of various systems of the human body
- Building an advanced awareness of scientific classification of animals
- Building knowledge of the internal parts of different groups of animals
- Connects adaptations in internal and external parts of animals with their environmental needs

### Botany (Biology)

- Shows awareness of the historical evolution of plants on earth
- Shows increasing awareness of plants found locally, in India and across the world
- Demonstrates interest in conducting various botanical experiments
- Demonstrates knowledge of the parts of a plant, and their functions
- Building a basic awareness of scientific classification of plants
- Shows awareness of various plant processes, including respiration and reproduction
- Shows awareness of interlinkages between plant and animal life, and other environmental factors (ecology)

### Enjoyment & Choice (Physical Geography, History, Civics & Economics)

- Demonstrates natural curiosity in the lives of people around the world, both today and over time (history)
- Demonstrates natural curiosity about physical geography (countries, maps, rivers, lakes, etc)

### Physical Geography (Physical Geography, History, Civics & Economics)

- Demonstrates an understanding of the evolution of the universe over time
- Demonstrates increasing comfort with various features of the world map (continents, countries, mountains etc)
- Locates and distinguishes various land forms and water forms
- Understands the linkage between the passage of time and the movement of the earth
- Understands the linkage between the geographical location of a place and its climate
- Understands the role of water in various geographic processes
- Understands the role of air and wind in various geographic processes

### Historical Studies (Physical Geography, History, Civics & Economics)

- Reads and constructs simple timelines
- Demonstrates an ability to correctly use vocabulary associated with time (CE, BCE, AD/BC)
- Demonstrates an understanding that people in different times and places fulfilled their needs in different ways
- Demonstrates increasing knowledge of important aspects of local (Karnataka) and Indian history
- Analyses the growth and features of historically important civilizations
- Demonstrates awareness of causes and consequences of great human migrations

### Scientific Literacy (Basic Scientific Skills)

- Talks about science, showing developing understanding of risks and benefits, and listens to the views of others on scientific topics.
- Demonstrates awareness of the importance of respecting living things and of managing the Earth’s resources responsibly.
- Demonstrates a developing understanding of science in the world around them.

### Civics (Physical Geography, History, Civics & Economics)

- Examines the rights and responsibilities of individuals to their social groups (family, peer group, class community, and so on)
- Compares the different forms of government
- Demonstrates an awareness of the varying roles of local, state and national government
- Examines the varying roles of the executive, the judiciary and the legislature
- Demonstrates increasing knowledge of the mechanisms of democracies  (elections, etc)
- Demonstrates familiarity with fundamental aspects of the Indian constitution

### Economics (Physical Geography, History, Civics & Economics)

- Demonstrates awareness of the inter-dependencies between various stakeholders in the economic system
- Examines the role of money in every day life
- Describes the relationship of price to supply and demand
- Shows an awareness of the role of taxation in the economic system


## Fine Arts and Performing Arts

### Art, Craft & Design (Creative Expression)

- Creates (2D) artwork directly from experiences across the curriculum
- Creates 3D models / handwork directly from experiences across the curriculum
- Uses a variety of available materials and media
- Shows understanding of basic colour theory, for example, which secondary colours are made from mixing primary colours.
- Solves simple design & engineering problems, working on their own and with others, using a degree of trial and error,
- Shows some understanding of the qualities and limitations of selected media, for example, uses pencil instead of a large brush, to make fine marks, uses chalks instead of pencil to fill larger spaces.
- Shows understanding of the concept of scale, for example, represents mountains as bigger than people.
- Shares thoughts and feelings by expressing personal views in response to the work of at least one artist and one designer.
- Demonstrates originality and invention in created artwork

### Dance & Movement (Creative Expression)

- Performs a range of simple, repeated, intentional movements and gestures.
- Chooses and explores ways of moving rhythmically, expressively and playfully.
- Rehearses and performs short dance sequences, working on their own and with others.
- Creates new dance movements and sequences using their dance repertoire, incorporating different speeds and levels, characters and emotions to add interest and variety.

### Drama (Creative Expression)

- Communicates ideas and feelings using aspects of voice, such as volume, expression and clarity.
- Communicates ideas and feelings using movement, for example, through body language, gestures, actions and posture.
- Communicates ideas and feelings using facial expressions, for example, to show happy, sad, surprised, angry, scared.
- Takes on a role within a drama, for example, a real or imagined situation, re-enactment of a story, a traditional tale.
- Shows understanding of how to work from a script, for example, a play, a story or a poem, by acting or speaking at the appropriate time.
- Creates a short drama using improvisation, from a given stimulus, and working collaboratively.
- Contributes towards the development of a drama, for example, by discussing aspects such as character, performance, or script, for example, what a character in a well-known story might say to another.

### Music (Creative Expression)

- Participates actively and uses his/her voice in singing activities from a range of styles and cultures
- Follows performance directions, for example, follows the group leader.
- Creates original songs with both lyric and tunes
- Demostrates interest in independently building skills in musical instruments of choice
- Volunteers to perform for an audience


## LITERACY & ENGLISH

### Enjoyment & choice (Writing)

- Actively chooses writing tasks and writes for enjoyment in a variety of contexts

### Tools for writing (Writing)

- Presents writing in a clear and legible way using images and other features as appropriate.
- Attempts to spell familiar words correctly. Uses knowledge of phonics and spelling strategies when spelling familiar and unfamiliar words.
- Uses basic punctuation (capital letters, full stops, commas, question marks, exclamation marks) appropriately.
- Constructs grammatically accurate sentences in written work.
- Creates texts with evidence of structure.
- Starts sentences in a variety of ways to engage the reader.
- Uses relevant and/or interesting vocabulary in written work, keeping in mind the form of the piece as well as the audience
- Captures the essence of a passage from a reference book and can summarize in own words

### Creating Texts (Writing)

- Writes to convey ideas, messages and information
- Writes about personal experiences in a logical order, using appropriate vocabulary to describe feelings, thoughts and events.
- Makes notes or uses other strategies to help plan writing and uses them to create new texts.
- Selects, organises and conveys information in different ways.
- Creates own texts, for example, stories, poems and plays, with recognisable features of genre.
- Creates interesting characters through their feelings and actions and physical description.
- Selects, organises and conveys information in different ways.

### Enjoyment and choice (Reading)

- Selects different texts regularly for enjoyment or for a specific purpose using, for example, cover, title, author, illustrator and/or blurb.
- Explains preferences for particular texts and authors.

### Tools for reading (Reading)

- Reads aloud a familiar piece of text adding expression and can show understanding.
- Reads an increasing number of common/high frequency words, key reading words, core topic words and words of personal significance.
- Uses a range of word recognition strategies independently.
- Decodes unknown words by locating and pronouncing familiar letter patterns and blends.
- Uses context clues to read and understand texts.

### Finding and using information (Reading)

- Finds information in a text to learn new things. Makes notes from reference books.
- Identifies and finds key information in fiction and non-fiction texts using content page, index, headings, sub-headings and diagrams to help locate information.

### Understanding &  analysing (Reading)

- Contributes to discussions about events, characters and ideas relevant to the text. Relates information and ideas from a text to personal experiences, after reading
- Identifies the main ideas of texts; Answers literal, inferential and evaluative questions about texts.
- Recognises the difference between fact and opinion.
- Offers own ideas about characters, writer’s use of language, structure and/or setting.
- Offers own ideas about the writer’s message and, when appropriate, relates these to personal experiences.

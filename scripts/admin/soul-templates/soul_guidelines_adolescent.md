# Guidelines Template — Adolescent (Ages 12-18)

> **Purpose:** This document defines the developmental areas, skills, and observable
> benchmarks that Coach Pepper uses to guide observations, interviews, and soul
> generation for students in the Adolescent program.
>
> **How to use:** Scan the discipline sections to identify relevant areas for a
> student. Use benchmarks as concrete indicators when observing, interviewing
> teachers, or generating the weekly soul narrative. Benchmarks are not checklists
> — they describe behaviors that may emerge at different paces for each child.
>
> **Editing:** This copy evolves with the student. You may add, remove, or annotate
> sections. Add custom notes under any heading with `> Note:` blockquotes.

---


## Personal, Social, Emotional & Moral Skills

### Motivation & Initiative (Personal Development)

- Sets challenging personal goals across various domains and actively works towards them
- Demonstrates intrinsic motivation in academic and extracurricular pursuits
- Takes initiative in proposing and leading projects or activities
- Perseveres through challenging tasks, showing resilience and determination

### Self-Management (Personal Development)

- Executes tasks to the best of their individual ability
- Effectively manages time, balancing academic, extracurricular, and personal responsibilities
- Demonstrates strong organizational skills in managing materials, assignments, and long-term projects
- Adapts strategies and approaches based on reflection and feedback

### Self-Awareness (Personal Development)

- Accurately assesses personal strengths and areas for growth
- Demonstrates an understanding of how personal actions impact others and the environment
- Articulates personal values and how they influence decision-making
- Shows awareness of own emotions and their impact on behavior and relationships

### Personal Responsibility (Personal Development)

- Takes ownership of personal choices and their consequences
- Demonstrates reliability in fulfilling commitments and meeting deadlines
- Strives for accuracy and quality in their work, minimising errors
- Takes responsibility for personal health and well-being (e.g., sleep, nutrition, exercise)

### Social Interaction & Communication (Social Development)

- Expresses ideas clearly and effectively in various contexts (one-on-one, small groups, presentations)
- Demonstrates active listening skills, showing engagement and understanding in conversations
- Adapts communication style appropriately for different audiences and purposes
- Uses digital tools responsibly and effectively for communication and collaboration

### Collaboration & Teamwork (Social Development)

- Works effectively in diverse groups, respecting different perspectives and contributions
- Assumes various roles in group work, including leadership when appropriate
- Resolves conflicts constructively, seeking win-win solutions

### Interpersonal Relationships (Social Development)

- Builds and maintains positive relationships with peers and adults
- Shows empathy and compassion towards others
- Navigates social situations with increasing confidence and authenticity

### Community Engagement (Social Development)

- Actively participates in school and community service projects
- Shows awareness of local and global issues and their interconnectedness
- Demonstrates a sense of responsibility towards the broader community

### Emotional Awareness & Expression (Emotional Development)

- Identifies and accurately labels a wide range of emotions in self and others
- Expresses emotions appropriately in various contexts
- Shows increasing ability to articulate complex or mixed emotions

### Emotional Regulation & Resilience (Emotional Development)

- Uses healthy strategies to manage strong emotions (e.g., stress, anger, anxiety)
- Demonstrates ability to self-soothe and recover from emotional upheavals
- Maintains emotional balance in challenging or high-pressure situations

### Empathy & Perspective Taking (Emotional Development)

- Demonstrates genuine concern for others' feelings and experiences
- Shows ability to imagine and understand others' perspectives, even when different from own
- Navigates interpersonal conflicts with emotional maturity
- Builds and maintains emotionally supportive friendships

### Growth Mindset (Emotional Development)

- Shows confidence in abilities while acknowledging areas for growth
- Accepts and learns from constructive criticism without being overly self-critical
- Seeks help and support when needed, demonstrating emotional self-care

### Ethical Decision-Making (Moral Development)

- Identifies ethical dilemmas in various contexts (personal, academic, societal)
- Applies ethical reasoning to make decisions, considering consequences and stakeholders
- Shows integrity by aligning actions with stated values and beliefs

### Justice & Equality (Moral Development)

- Demonstrates awareness of social inequalities and their systemic causes
- Shows commitment to fairness and equality in personal interactions and broader contexts
- Advocates for the rights and fair treatment of others

### Environmental Stewardship (Moral Development)

- Shows understanding of human impact on the environment
- Demonstrates commitment to sustainable practices in daily life
- Participates in or initiates environmental conservation efforts
- Considers environmental implications in decision-making processes

### Global Citizenship (Moral Development)

- Demonstrates awareness of global interconnectedness and interdependence
- Shows interest in and knowledge of global issues and diverse cultures
- Considers the global impact of local actions and personal choices

### Civic Responsibility (Moral Development)

- Shows a sense of duty towards family, school, and community
- Volunteers for service activities without expectation of reward
- Takes on leadership roles in service or community-oriented projects


## Economic Enterprise

### Business Planning & Strategy (Entrepreneurship)

- Develops a clear and coherent business concept or idea
- Conducts basic market research to understand customer needs and preferences
- Creates a simple business plan outlining goals, target market, and basic financials
- Adapts strategies based on feedback and changing conditions

### Financial Literacy & Management (Entrepreneurship)

- Understands and applies basic budgeting principles to the enterprise
- Tracks income and expenses accurately using appropriate tools
- Creates a basic book of accounts
- Calculates basic financial metrics (e.g., profit, loss, revenue)
- Makes informed decisions about pricing products or services

### Sales & Marketing (Entrepreneurship)

- Develops and implements basic marketing strategies to promote the enterprise
- Communicates effectively with customers and addresses their concerns
- Creates appealing and informative promotional materials
- Utilizes appropriate physical and digital platforms to market products or services

### Operations (Entrepreneurship)

- Organizes and manages resources (time, materials, people) effectively
- Shares tasks and responsibilities appropriately within the team
- Identifies and solves operational problems as they arise
- Collaborates effectively with team members and resolves conflicts constructively


## Creative Arts

### Enjoyment & Choice (Expression)

- Explores personal identity and values through creative artistic endeavors
- Connects creative works to academic subjects and real-world issues
- Integrates multiple art forms in complex, self-directed projects

### Visual Arts (Expression)

- Explores various visual mediums to express ideas and emotions
- Develops personal style and articulates the meaning behind artwork
- Demonstrates critical thinking in analyzing and interpreting visual art

### Performing Arts (Expression)

- Expresses ideas and emotions through music, drama, or movement
- Collaborates effectively in group performances or productions
- Develops technical skills in chosen performing art form

### Digital Arts (Expression)

- Creates digital content to communicate ideas innovatively
- Uses technology tools responsibly and creatively for self-expression
- Explores the intersection of technology and traditional art forms


## MATHEMATICS

### Number Sense & Operations (Number Sense & Algebraic Thinking)

- Understands and performs operations with positive and negative numbers
- Applies order of operations to solve multi-step problems
- Understands and applies number theory concepts (factors, multiples, prime factorization)
- Understands and uses scientific notation for very large or small numbers
- Applies concepts of exponents and roots
- Uses estimation strategies to check reasonableness of results
- Understands and applies the concept of absolute value

### Fractions, Decimals, Percentages, Ratios (Number Sense & Algebraic Thinking)

- Converts between fractions, decimals, and percentages
- Performs operations with fractions, decimals, and percentages
- Solves real-world problems involving percentages (e.g., discounts, interest)
- Understands and applies ratios to real-world problems
- Understands and applies concepts of direct and inverse proportions

### Algebra (Number Sense & Algebraic Thinking)

- Simplifies and evaluates algebraic expressions
- Understands and uses variables to represent unknown quantities
- Translates word problems into algebraic expressions and equations
- Solves systems of linear equations algebraically
- Factorizes algebraic expressions
- Solves quadratic equations

### Functions, Graphs (Patterns, Functions & Data)

- Understands the concept of a function as a relationship between variables
- Interprets and creates graphs of linear functions
- Identifies and describes patterns in sequences and functions
- Uses graphs to represent and analyze data sets

### Statistics (Patterns, Functions & Data)

- Collects, organizes, and represents data using various methods
- Calculates and interprets measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode)
- Understands the effect of outliers on a dataset
- Analyzes and interprets data from graphs and charts
- Chooses appropriate graphical representations for different types of data
- Understands the concept of sampling and its importance in making inferences
- Recognizes the difference between correlation and causation

### Probability (Patterns, Functions & Data)

- Understands and calculates theoretical and experimental probability
- Understands and uses the concepts of independent and dependent events
- Calculates probabilities of compound events using tree diagrams, tables, or formulas
- Engages with real-world applications of probability

### Geometry & Measurement (Geometry & Spatial Reasoning)

- Identifies, describes and analyzes properties of 2D and 3D shapes
- Understands and applies properties of angles to a range of problems
- Analyzes and proves triangle congruence and similarity
- Analyzes and proves various properties of circles
- Calculates area and perimeter of various 2D shapes
- Calculates surface area and volume of various 3D shapes
- Understands and applies the Pythagorean theorem

### Coordinate Geometry (Geometry & Spatial Reasoning)

- Plots points and lines on a coordinate plane
- Determines slopes of lines and understands parallel relationships
- Solves systems of linear equations geometrically

### Spatial Reasoning (Geometry & Spatial Reasoning)

- Identifies and creates symmetrical figures
- Performs and describes transformations (rotations, reflections, translations)
- Applies transformations to solve geometric problems

### Problem Solving (Mathematical Reasoning & Application)

- Applies various strategies to solve new kinds of problems
- Explains mathematical thinking and justifies solutions
- Formulates own mathematical problems and attempts to solve them
- Identifies and corrects errors in mathematical work

### Applications (Mathematical Reasoning & Application)

- Uses mathematics to model and solve real-world problems
- Applies mathematical concepts across different subject areas
- Creates and interprets mathematical models of physical phenomena
- Uses technology appropriately to explore and solve mathematical problems


## ENGLISH COMMUNICATION

### Oral Communication (Speaking & Listening)

- Speaks effectively in a range of collaborative discussions
- Presents claims and findings with relevant evidence and sound reasoning
- Adapts speech to a variety of contexts (1-1, small group, large group) and communicative tasks (information, persuasion, debate etc)
- Uses appropriate eye contact, volume, and clear pronunciation

### Listening & Comprehension (Speaking & Listening)

- Able to recall and summarize key points from oral communication
- Asks appropriate clarifying questions to help analyze ideas oral communication
- Evaluates a speaker's point of view, reasoning, and use of evidence
- Identifies and evaluates persuasive techniques in speeches and presentations

### Drama & Performance (Speaking & Listening)

- Interprets and performs dramatic texts with appropriate expression
- Uses voice modulation and gestures effectively in performances
- Collaborates with peers to create and perform original dramatic pieces
- Analyzes how actors use voice and movement to create characters

### Word Acquisition & Usage (Vocabulary & Grammar)

- Determines the meaning of unknown words using context clues and reference materials
- Understands and uses figurative language, word relationships, and nuances
- Acquires and accurately uses grade-appropriate general, academic and domain-specific words
- Choose vocabulary that expresses ideas precisely and concisely

### Grammar & Usage (Vocabulary & Grammar)

- Demonstrates command of standard English grammar and usage
- Uses various types of sentences and clauses to convey specific meanings
- Applies knowledge of language to make effective choices for meaning or style in different contexts

### Understanding & Interpreting texts (Comprehension & Analysis)

- Identifies main ideas, themes, and supporting details in various text types
- Makes inferences and draws conclusions based on textual evidence
- Evaluates quality and provenance of sources cited or used in a text
- Analyzes how language choices contribute to meaning and tone

### Genre & Literary Devices (Comprehension & Analysis)

- Recognizes and analyzes features of different genres (e.g., fiction, non-fiction, poetry, drama)
- Identifies and explains the effect of literary devices (e.g., metaphor, simile, personification)
- Examines how structure contributes to meaning in texts
- Evaluates the impact of word choice on tone and mood

### Critical Reading (Comprehension & Analysis)

- Distinguishes between fact and opinion in informational texts
- Evaluates arguments and specific claims in texts
- Identifies bias and considers multiple perspectives
- Relates texts to personal experiences, ideas, and other texts

### Writing process (Writing)

- Plans, drafts, revises, and edits writing effectively
- Develops and strengthens writing by focusing on purpose and audience
- Uses feedback constructively to improve writing
- Applies appropriate writing conventions (punctuation, spelling)

### Range of creative expression (Writing)

- Writes clear and coherent fictional narratives with well-structured event sequences
- Produces informative/explanatory texts to examine topics and convey ideas
- Constructs argumentative/persuasive texts with clear reasons and relevant evidence
- Creates poetry using figurative language and poetic devices
- Creates or chooses appropriate illustrations for various forms of written work

### Technology (Writing)

- Uses technology to produce and publish writing across a range of formats
- Integrates information into writing while avoiding plagiarism
- Cites sources, including tech and non-tech sources


## HINDI

### Listening and talking (Listening & Talking)

- Uses some polite social terms to begin and end interactions.
- Building an increasingly rich vocabulary in the target language, and uses new words regularly
- Shares personal information on familiar topics such as expressing likes or dislikes with increasing confidence and accuracy.
- Presents to the group fluently in the target language
- Sustains conversations long enough to demonstrate understanding and use of basic structures and familiar vocabulary in different contexts.
- Can orally translate phrases from the target language into English or vice-versa

### Tools for writing (Writing)

- Writes familiar words and spell them correctly.
- Constructs grammatically accurate sentences in written work.
- Organizes written work in a logical manner.
- Uses relevant and/or interesting vocabulary in written work. keeping in mind the form of the piece as well as the audience
- Captures the essence of a passage from a reference book and can summarize in own words

### Creating Texts (Writing)

- Writes to convey ideas, messages and information
- Writes to share own experiences and feelings
- Creates original fictional narratives
- Creates original non-fictional content in the target language
- Translates short passages from English to the target language, and vice-versa
- Translates long passages / books from English to the target language, and vice-versa

### Tools for reading (Reading)

- Reads an increasing number of common/high frequency words, key reading words, core topic words and words of personal significance.
- Reads short passages fluently
- Reads long passages / short books with increasing comfort
- Uses context clues to read and understand the meaning of texts in the target language containing unfamiliar words.

### Understanding, analysing and evaluating (Reading)

- Asks and answers questions about ideas in a text, after reading; Answers questions to help predict what will happen next, after reading
- Identifies the main ideas of texts; Answers comprehension questions about texts.
- Contributes to discussions about events, characters and ideas relevant to the text.

### Listening for information (Listening & Talking)

- Participates actively in songs, rhymes, stories and poems in the target language.
- Demonstrates understanding of a range of classroom instructions
- Demonstrates understanding of familiar words and phrases in increasingly full sentences / phrases

### Listening and talking (Listening & Talking)

- Uses some polite social terms to begin and end interactions.
- Building an increasingly rich vocabulary in the target language, and uses new words regularly
- Shares personal information on familiar topics. Speaks increasingly in fuller sentences/phrases.
- Presents to the group in the target language
- Sustains conversations long enough to demonstrate understanding and use of basic structures and familiar vocabulary in different contexts.
- Can orally translate phrases from the target language into English or vice-versa

### Tools for writing (Writing)

- Writes identifiable letters representing sounds correctly,
- Attempts to write familiar words and spell them correctly.
- Constructs grammatically accurate sentences in written work.
- Organizes written work in a logical manner.
- Uses relevant and/or interesting vocabulary in written work. keeping in mind the form of the piece as well as the audience
- Captures the essence of a passage from a reference book and can summarize in own words

### Creating Texts (Writing)

- Creates simple new texts based around the familiar words and phrases practised during talking, listening and reading activities
- Writes to convey ideas, messages and information
- Writes to share own experiences and feelings
- Creates original fictional narratives

- Translates short passages from English to the target language, and vice-versa

### Tools for reading (Reading)

- Identifies the different single sounds made by letters.
- Identifies sounds made by a combination of letters (including combination of vowels and consonants)
- Reads an increasing number of common/high frequency words, key reading words, core topic words and words of personal significance.
- Uses knowledge of sounds, letters and patterns to read words.
- Uses context clues to read and understand the meaning of texts in the target language containing unfamiliar words.
- Reads aloud familiar texts with attention to simple punctuation. Adds expression to show understanding when reading familiar texts.

### Understanding, analysing and evaluating (Reading)

- Asks and answers questions about ideas in a text, after reading; Answers questions to help predict what will happen next, after reading
- Identifies the main ideas of texts; Answers comprehension questions about texts.
- Contributes to discussions about events, characters and ideas relevant to the text.


## Sciences

### Scientific Inquiry (Core Scientific Skills)

- Formulates testable questions and hypotheses based on observations
- Designs and conducts experiments using appropriate tools and safety procedures
- Collects, records, and organizes data accurately using various methods
- Analyzes results, draws evidence-based conclusions, and communicates findings effectively

### Scientific Models (Core Scientific Skills)

- Creates and uses models to explain complex scientific concepts
- Evaluates the strengths and limitations of scientific models
- Constructs explanations using evidence, scientific reasoning, and theories

### Science in the Real World (Core Scientific Skills)

- Uses scientific principles to design solutions to real-world problems
- Interprets cause and effect relationships in scientific systems
- Applies interdisciplinary knowledge to understand complex issues (e.g., climate change)
- Assesses the impact of scientific discoveries and technological solutions on society and the environment

### Matter and Energy (Core Scientific Knowledge)

- Describes the properties and behavior of matter in different states
- Explains energy transformations and conservation in various systems
- Understands the structure of atoms, elements, and the basics of the Periodic Table
- Recognizes patterns in chemical reactions and their role in natural processes

### Life Processes (Core Scientific Knowledge)

- Explains the structure and function of cells as the basic units of life
- Describes key biological processes including photosynthesis and respiration
- Understands the organization of specialized cells into tissues, organs, and systems
- Recognizes the basics of genetics and inheritance

### Earth Systems (Core Scientific Knowledge)

- Understands the interactions between Earth's systems (atmosphere, hydrosphere, geosphere, biosphere)
- Describes the water cycle and its impact on weather and climate
- Explains the carbon cycle and its role in climate change
- Recognizes the impact of human activities on ecosystems and global climate

### History of Science (Core Scientific Knowledge)

- Recognizes key milestones and figures in the development of scientific ideas
- Understands how scientific knowledge evolves over time through observation, experimentation, and new discoveries
- Recognizes the interconnection between scientific advancements and societal changes throughout history


## Social Sciences

### Historical Time & Context (History)

- Creates and explains timelines of important historical events
- Understands how historians divide history into different time periods
- Explains the structure of society in a given historical context
- Compares events from different time periods to identify changes and similarities

### Research & Critical Thinking Skills (History)

- Finds and evaluates different sources of historical information for reliability and credibility
- Formulates relevant historical questions as a basis for independent study
- Identifies opinions, biases, vested interests, and value judgments in historical sources
- Applies various methods to test and verify historical claims

### Understanding Different Perspectives (History)

- Explains how the time and place people lived in shaped their views and actions
- Describes how different groups of people might have viewed the same historical event
- Avoids judging past actions by today's standards, trying to understand them in their historical setting

### Explaining Historical Events (History)

- Develops inferences about historical events using evidence, including primary sources
- Explains the cause and effect relationship between events in history
- Evaluates why some historical events were more important than others
- Effectively communicates well-reasoned arguments about history in writing, speaking, and other forms

### Structure & Function of Government (Civics & Political Science)

- Explains the roles of local, state, and national levels of government
- Understands the functions of various branches of the government, along with checks and balances
- Describes the structure and functions of local government (e.g., city council, mayor)
- Compares the responsibilities of local government with those of state and central governments

### Laws & the Constitution (Civics & Political Science)

- Examines the origins and purposes of laws, rules, and key constitutional provisions
- Understands the role of the constitution in protecting individual rights and limiting government power
- Understands how international agreements impact national and local governance

### Civic Engagement (Civics & Political Science)

- Identifies ways to participate in local community decision-making (e.g.,meeting local councillor, police etc)
- Engages with volunteer opportunities and community service projects in the local area
- compare deliberative processes used by a wide variety of groups in various settings
- Applies civic virtues and democratic principles in school & related settings

### Analyzing Local & Broader Issues (Civics & Political Science)

- Identifies and researches key issues affecting the local community
- Discusses the effects of local decisions on various community members and groups
- Describes the connection between local, national, and global economies

### Analyzing Physical & Human Geography (Geography)

- Describes major physical features of Earth's surface (e.g., mountains, oceans, deserts)
- Explains how physical processes shape Earth's landscapes and ecosystems
- Identifies patterns of human settlement and how they relate to physical geography
- Understands the distribution and characteristics of world cultures and populations

### Understanding & applying geographical knowledge (Geography)

- Reads and interprets different types of maps (e.g., physical, political, thematic)
- Understands and applies basic geographic concepts (e.g., latitude, longitude, scale)
- Uses geographic knowledge to explain current events and global issues
- Compares and contrasts different regions based on geographic characteristics
- Analyzes how geography influences economic activities and trade

### Exploring Human-Environment interactions (Geography)

- Describes how the physical environment influences human activities and settlements
- Explains how human activities modify the physical environment
- Understands the concept of natural resources and their distribution
- Discusses environmental challenges and conservation efforts in different regions

### Basic Economic Concepts (Economics)

- Explains the concepts of scarcity, choice, and opportunity cost
- Describes how supply and demand affect prices in a market economy
- Understands the role of producers and consumers in the economy
- Identifies different types of resources (natural, human, capital) and their economic importance

### Economic Systems & Institutions (Economics)

- Compares different economic systems (e.g., market, command, mixed economies)
- Describes the role of banks and other financial institutions in the economy
- Understands the basic functions of government in the economy (e.g., providing public goods, regulations)
- Explains the concept of entrepreneurship and its role in economic growth

### Global Economic Connections (Economics)

- Describes how trade between countries affects their economies
- Understands the concept of exchange rates and how they impact international trade
- Identifies major trading partners of their country and the goods commonly exchanged
- Discusses how global events can impact local and national economies
